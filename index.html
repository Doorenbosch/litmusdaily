The Mechanism

November's $3.5 billion ETF outflow wasn't just profit-taking—it was the institutional bid stepping away. Bitcoin's 6% overnight decline to $85,945 reveals what happens when algorithmic momentum strategies all hit the same exit. The reflexive loop that drove prices higher has shifted into reverse: falling prices trigger systematic selling, which produces more falling prices.

The Complication

However, the picture isn't uniformly bearish. Stablecoin supply continues expanding—USDT market cap hit a new all-time high this week. This divergence between price weakness and liquidity growth has historically preceded accumulation phases. Someone is preparing to buy; they're just not buying yet.

The Behavioral Layer

What we're witnessing is classic narrative exhaustion. The "institutional adoption" story that powered the rally has been fully absorbed—priced in, as they say. Markets need new stories to move, and the current environment offers only macro uncertainty. In the absence of a fresh catalyst, the path of least resistance is down.

The Forward View
